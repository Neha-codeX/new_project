Raw Prompt: {Create a highly modern, sleek, intuitive, and visually stunning website about the degrading health of our planet caused by all major types of pollution.

The website should feel premium, futuristic, and immersive, with a strong environmental storytelling experience. It should be designed for maximum clarity, user engagement, and educational impact. Make the UI elegant, minimal, and polished, with smooth transitions, subtle animations, glassmorphism/neumorphism-inspired cards, clean typography, and a refined color palette inspired by nature, earth, ocean, smoke, and warning tones.

Main goal:
Build a website that helps users understand how pollution is damaging the planet’s health, while making the experience interactive, emotional, and easy to explore.

Core concept:
The website should present “Planet Health” like a living body or patient dashboard, showing how different pollution types affect air, water, soil, noise, light, plastic, chemical, and thermal balance. Each type of pollution must have its own visualization, animation, or simulation so users can clearly understand the impact.

Design direction:
- Very modern, sleek, premium, and intuitive
- Dark mode by default with elegant environmental gradients
- Optional light mode with soft eco-inspired tones
- Smooth scrolling and parallax motion
- Glassmorphism panels with subtle blur and depth
- Clean hero section with strong headline and immersive background motion
- High-end editorial layout mixed with data-driven UI
- Responsive for desktop, tablet, and mobile
- Accessible, readable, and easy to navigate

Website structure:

1. Hero section
- Large cinematic headline such as:
  “The Planet Is Running a Fever”
  or
  “Tracking the Health of Our Planet”
- Subheading explaining that pollution is damaging Earth’s systems
- Animated background showing Earth, smoke, waves, particles, or a living planet pulse
- Strong CTA buttons:
  - Explore Pollution Types
  - View Planet Health Dashboard
- Add a subtle scroll indicator

2. Planet health dashboard
Create a central dashboard that represents the Earth’s condition like a medical or environmental diagnostic panel.
Include:
- Overall Planet Health Score
- Air Quality Index
- Water Health
- Soil Health
- Biodiversity Impact
- Waste Load
- Temperature Stress
- Noise Stress
- Visual meter or orbital ring around a planet icon

3. Pollution categories section
Create interactive cards for each major pollution type:
- Air Pollution
- Water Pollution
- Soil Pollution
- Noise Pollution
- Light Pollution
- Plastic Pollution
- Chemical Pollution
- Thermal Pollution
- Radiation Pollution

- Severity indicator
- Hover animation
- “Explore simulation” action

4. Simulations / visualizations for each pollution type
This is the most important part.

For every pollution type, create a unique visualization or simulation:

Air Pollution
- Animated city skyline with smoke, smog layers, traffic emissions, and floating particles
- Show lung/air-quality-style visualization
- Include AQI-style meter that worsens as emissions rise

Water Pollution
- Animated river/ocean scene with floating waste, oil slicks, and discoloration spread
- Water clarity changes over time
- Include ripple, contamination, and marine-life impact animation

Soil Pollution
- Cross-section soil visualization
- Show chemicals seeping into ground layers
- Show reduced plant growth and contaminated roots
- Use layered earth diagram

Noise Pollution
- Soundwave-based simulation
- Urban environment with vibrating UI elements
- Visual pressure rings expanding from traffic, industry, and construction sources

Light Pollution
- Night sky simulation with washed-out stars and city glow
- Demonstrate how excessive brightness hides the stars and affects ecosystems

Plastic Pollution
- Ocean and land waste accumulation simulation
- Floating plastic objects, microplastic particles, and landfill overflow
- Show wildlife entanglement impact in a sensitive, educational way

Chemical Pollution
- Hazard map / laboratory spill style visualization
- Chemical diffusion spreading across air/water/soil layers
- Use caution-style scientific visuals

Thermal Pollution
- Heat map visualization with warming water bodies and temperature overlays
- Show ecosystem stress from excess industrial heat

Radiation Pollution
- Geiger-style visual elements
- Energy field / radiation rings visualization
- Present it as scientific and educational, not sensational

All simulations should feel polished, lightweight, and interactive. They should not be childish. They should be visually sophisticated, data-driven, and educational.
