ORIGIN

Where It Begins

Industry, transportation, agriculture, and waste management are the primary engines of planetary pollution. Over 70% of all pollution is anthropogenic — generated by human systems designed without considering their long-term impact on Earth's biochemistry.

SPREAD

How It Propagates

Pollution does not stay where it starts. Atmospheric currents carry particulates across continents. Ocean gyres concentrate plastic from thousands of miles away. Groundwater carries chemical contaminants through aquifer networks spanning entire regions.

🧬
ECOSYSTEM IMPACT

What It Does to Life

Pollutants disrupt hormonal systems, damage DNA, bleach coral reefs, acidify oceans, collapse food webs, and push species toward extinction at 1,000 times the natural background rate. The web of life is unraveling.
